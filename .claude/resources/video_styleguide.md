# the creator's Video Style Guide

A visual and audio identity for all video content - shorts, long-form, and AI avatar videos.

---

## Core Aesthetic

**Light, clean, confident.** The visual identity feels like a premium tech product - minimal but warm, authoritative but approachable. Think Apple marketing meets modern SaaS design.

The overall mood is **"smart colleague presenting something important"** - professional, focused, optimistic.

---

## Color Palette

**Primary Background**: Soft pale red gradient to white or light gray
The signature look. Warm but not aggressive. Creates a distinctive, recognizable canvas that stands out from the sea of dark-mode tech content.

**Primary Text**: Dark gray (not pure black)
Softer than pure black, more sophisticated. Easy on the eyes while maintaining strong contrast.

**Accent - Brand**: Bright red
The signature color. Used for emphasis, highlights, key words, and call-to-action elements. Creates visual anchors and draws the eye to important information.

**Overlay Elements**: Semi-transparent white
Cards, boxes, and containers use frosted white with subtle shadows. Creates layered depth without heaviness.

**Secondary Accent**: Light gray
For supporting elements, subtle backgrounds, and visual separation.

---

## Typography

**Primary Font**: DM Sans
Clean, geometric, modern. Works at all sizes. Professional without being cold.

**Text Characteristics**:
- Headlines are bold (700 weight) and dominant - they own the frame
- Body text uses regular weight with comfortable spacing
- ALL CAPS used sparingly for short labels or single impactful words
- Mixed case preferred for readability

**Text Sizing Philosophy**:
- When in doubt, go bigger
- Key messages should be readable if the video were paused and viewed as a thumbnail
- Mobile viewers are the design constraint - if it's hard to read on a phone, it's too small

**Text Animation**:
- Words appear with purpose, not decoration
- Preferred: word-by-word reveal synced to speech, with current word in bright red
- Clean fade-ins with subtle position shifts
- Avoid: letter-by-letter typewriter effects
- Avoid: bouncy or elastic animations

---

## Motion Language

**Overall Feel**: Confident and deliberate. Nothing bounces, wobbles, or overshoots. Movement has intention and settles cleanly.

**Easing**: Always ease-out or ease-in-out. Never linear. Elements arrive with confidence.

**Speed**: Brisk but not rushed. Transitions complete in 0.2-0.4 seconds.

**Intro Treatment**:
- Brief brand moment (2 seconds maximum)
- Subtle motion - gentle scale, soft blur resolve, or smooth fade
- Content emerges cleanly, gaining clarity and presence

**Transitions Between Sections**:
- Prefer cuts for pacing
- When motion is needed: simple slides or fades
- Nothing flashy or attention-grabbing - transitions serve the content

**Text Motion**:
- Text enters with subtle opacity and position shift
- Text exits by fading, not flying
- Grouped elements animate together unless deliberately emphasizing sequence

---

## Captions and On-Screen Text

**Caption Style**:
- Large enough to read on mobile without squinting
- Positioned in lower third, but not buried at the very bottom
- Dark gray text on semi-transparent white background card
- Rounded corners on caption backgrounds (consistent with overlay style)

**Caption Animation**:
- Word-by-word highlight preferred
- Current word in bright red, previous words in dark gray
- Smooth transitions between words, synced to speech rhythm

**Emphasis**:
- Key words highlighted in bright red
- Important numbers or statistics can get a brief size bump
- One emphasis technique at a time - never stack effects

---

## Visual Overlays and Graphics

**Card Style**:
- Semi-transparent white backgrounds
- Rounded corners throughout (consistent radius)
- Subtle drop shadows for depth and lift
- Clean edges, no borders unless using bright red for emphasis

**Lower Thirds**:
- Minimal design - name and title only
- Matches the frosted white card style
- Appears briefly at video start

**Supporting Graphics**:
- Screenshots and diagrams float on white cards with rounded corners
- Subtle shadow to lift them off the background
- Slight scale animation on entry (95% to 100%)

**Diagrams and Illustrations**:
- Clean line art in dark gray or bright red
- White or pale backgrounds
- Animated drawing effect for reveals works well

**Screen Recordings**:
- Framed in white card with rounded corners
- Subtle shadow to separate from background
- Cursor highlighted for visibility

---

## Background Treatments

**Gradient Direction**:
- Typically top-left (soft red) to bottom-right (white/light gray)
- Can vary for visual interest but always within the pale red to white spectrum

**Geometric Elements**:
- Thin lines in bright red or light gray
- Minimal, purposeful - never cluttered
- Subtle patterns that add texture without competing

**Negative Space**:
- Essential to the aesthetic
- 20-30% of frame should breathe
- Busy compositions feel off-brand

---

## Audio Identity

**Voice Treatment**:
- Clean and present, not over-processed
- Subtle compression for consistent levels
- No artificial reverb - direct and professional

**Music Bed**:
- Light, positive, modern
- Unobtrusive - supports without competing
- Fades during key points, returns during transitions
- No heavy bass or dramatic builds

**Sound Design**:
- Subtle, soft sounds for transitions
- Gentle "pop" or light whoosh for text reveals (optional, use sparingly)
- Avoid: notification sounds, cartoon effects, dramatic stingers

---

## Platform-Specific Notes

**YouTube Shorts / TikTok / Instagram Reels**:
- First frame must hook - no slow intros
- Captions mandatory (most watch muted)
- Text must be readable at thumbnail size

**LinkedIn**:
- Slightly more measured pacing acceptable
- Professional tone reinforced by the clean aesthetic
- Captions still essential

**YouTube Long-Form**:
- Thumbnail frames should echo video style
- Intro still under 5 seconds
- Consistent visual language with shorts

---

## What to Avoid

- Dark/black backgrounds (off-brand)
- Neon colors or heavy saturation
- Complex gradients with multiple colors
- Excessive motion, particles, or effects
- Emojis in captions or overlays
- Small text that's hard to read on mobile
- Cluttered compositions - when in doubt, simplify
- Pure black text - use dark gray
- Sharp corners - maintain rounded aesthetic

---

## The Guiding Question

Before any creative decision, ask: **"Does this look like it belongs next to my thumbnails?"**

Visual consistency across thumbnails, shorts, and long-form creates brand recognition. A viewer should recognize your content before they see your name.
